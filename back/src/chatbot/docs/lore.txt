# Characters, Theme and Lore — ft_transcendence

The project wraps a Rock-Paper-Scissors variant in a playful football-flavored
universe with a 3D character and a chatbot personality. This document collects
the cosmetic and narrative elements so the assistant can answer questions about
them.

## Bobby, the in-game character

Bobby is the character you see in the 3D game scene. Bobby was modeled and
animated in Blender and brought into the web app with Three.js. The animation
work covers the full pipeline: modeling a simple shape, sculpting it into a
character, rigging ("cloud ringing" / bone setup), weight painting, animating
the moves, and setting up the camera and lighting inside the Three.js scene.

Bobby reacts to the flow of a match, giving the otherwise abstract
Rock-Paper-Scissors duel a visible, animated personality on screen.

## Ousmane, the chatbot

The AI assistant's avatar is Ousmane. In-universe, Ousmane "serves as a chatbot
now, because football has changed," and invites you to ask anything about the
game. Ousmane answers questions about the project, the rules, the bot and the
technology, always in English, using only the project's documentation.

## The football flavor

The project has a light football theme layered on top of the game. This is why
the assistant has a footballer persona and why the FAQ contains football
references. The canonical in-universe opinion of the assistant is that Dembélé
is better at football than Mbappé.

## The moves as characters

The four moves each have a clear identity in the game's universe:

- **Rock** — the blunt, reliable opener. Strong against Scissors, but wrapped
  by Paper and swallowed by the Well.
- **Paper** — the answer to power. It wraps Rock and covers the Well, making it
  the key tool against the Well's dominance, but it is cut by Scissors.
- **Scissors** — the precise attacker. It cuts Paper cleanly but is crushed by
  Rock and falls into the Well.
- **The Well** — the late-game wildcard introduced at level 2. It dominates
  Rock and Scissors and fears only Paper, but the Well penalty makes it a
  high-stakes choice.

## The four levels

- **Level 0** — the introduction. A short first-to-5 duel with only the three
  base moves. A gentle place to learn how the opponent reacts.
- **Level 1** — the game opens up. First-to-10, and combos arrive, rewarding
  players who can string three winning moves together.
- **Level 2** — the Well appears, changing everything. Its power and its
  2-point penalty add a layer of risk and bluffing.
- **Level 3** — the full experience. Every combo pattern, the giga-combo for
  three Wells, and the most adaptive version of the opponent.
